# Idea Menu — vetted for fastest path to first paying customer

Pick one (or bring your own — clear it so no two people build the same thing). These are
ranked by how quickly you can plausibly collect **real money**, not by how impressive they
sound. Each lists: who pays, the pricing model, the fastest dollar test, build difficulty,
and the trap to avoid.

Difficulty = how hard to reach a *sellable* slice in ~48h (★ easy → ★★★ hard).

---

## Tier A — fastest to a real charge

### 1. Niche AI "done-for-you" service (concierge MVP)
- **Who pays:** SMBs / agencies in one vertical (e.g. dentists, real-estate, law firms).
- **Model:** $X one-time or monthly retainer.
- **Fastest $ test:** Stripe Payment Link + a one-page offer. Deliver the first orders by
  hand; the AI is half-automation behind the scenes.
- **Difficulty:** ★ — the "product" is the offer, not the code.
- **Trap:** picking too broad an audience. Go narrow enough to name 20 real prospects.

### 2. AI document/report generator (pay-per-output)
- **Who pays:** founders, analysts, consultants who need a polished artifact now.
- **Model:** pay-per-report or credit packs ($5–$49 each).
- **Fastest $ test:** one input form → one great output → Stripe before download.
- **Difficulty:** ★★
- **Trap:** making it general. "Generate any report" sells worse than "Generate a VC-ready
  competitor teardown."

### 3. Restaurant / local-business phone agent
- **Who pays:** local owners who miss calls = miss revenue.
- **Model:** $99–$299/mo.
- **Fastest $ test:** record a demo call against their real number, DM/cold-call the owner,
  take a setup deposit.
- **Difficulty:** ★★ (use a voice API; the sale is the hard part, not the tech).
- **Trap:** building the whole telephony stack before talking to one owner.

## Tier B — strong SaaS, slightly longer to first dollar

### 4. AI code-review bot
- **Who pays:** dev teams / solo devs.
- **Model:** $/seat SaaS or usage credits.
- **Fastest $ test:** GitHub Marketplace listing or a "install + first 5 reviews free, then
  pay" flow on a few open-source repos' maintainers.
- **Difficulty:** ★★★
- **Trap:** competing on "we review everything." Pick one painful thing (e.g. catches
  security regressions) and nail it.

### 5. E-commerce product-page / SEO generator
- **Who pays:** Shopify / Etsy sellers.
- **Model:** $19–$49/mo or credits.
- **Fastest $ test:** Shopify app listing (trial → paid), or a standalone tool with a
  founder discount.
- **Difficulty:** ★★
- **Trap:** output that reads like AI. Sellers churn fast if Google doesn't reward it.

### 6. AI SDR / outbound agent (single channel)
- **Who pays:** B2B founders, agencies.
- **Model:** monthly + per-meeting.
- **Fastest $ test:** pre-sell one pilot at a flat fee; run it semi-manually.
- **Difficulty:** ★★
- **Trap:** deliverability and spam. Keep it to *one* clean channel for the demo.

### 7. Recruiting / resume screener
- **Who pays:** recruiters, hiring managers, small agencies.
- **Model:** per-role or per-candidate.
- **Fastest $ test:** "score 50 resumes against this JD" → pay-per-role checkout.
- **Difficulty:** ★★
- **Trap:** bias/compliance optics — keep humans in the loop and say so.

## Tier C — bigger swing, validate with pre-orders first

### 8. Real-estate deal analyzer
- **Who pays:** small investors.
- **Model:** $29–$99/mo.
- **Fastest $ test:** waitlist with a $5 deposit; show one killer auto-analysis.
- **Difficulty:** ★★
- **Trap:** data sourcing (comps/listings) is the real work — fake it for the demo, but
  be honest in the pitch.

### 9. AI meeting / notes agent
- **Who pays:** teams.
- **Model:** $/seat.
- **Fastest $ test:** free→paid upgrade gate after N meetings.
- **Difficulty:** ★★★ (crowded — differentiate by vertical or by one killer feature).
- **Trap:** competing head-on with incumbents. Win a niche (e.g. sales-call coaching).

### 10. "Any API → MCP server" generator
- **Who pays:** developers building AI agents.
- **Model:** one-time or credits.
- **Fastest $ test:** "generate my MCP server" → pay to download/export.
- **Difficulty:** ★★
- **Trap:** devs expect free/OSS. Charge for convenience + hosting, not the code.

## Choosing well (read before you pick)

1. **Can you name 20 real people who'd pay this week?** If not, pick a different audience.
2. **Can you collect a dollar without finishing the product?** (concierge / pre-sale) The
   more "yes," the better the hackathon idea.
3. **Do you have an unfair edge?** Domain access, a warm lead, an existing audience, a
   channel. Lean into it. (Yemi: your TNA/GovCon and agency relationships are unfair edges.)
